## 8. Color Rules

- Royal Blue `#3D5FA0` is the signature color.
- Royal Blue must appear in each visual cycle.
- Royal Blue is used for primary panels, CTA zones, and major brand surfaces.
- Sky Blue is used only as a soft supporting blue.
- Navy is used only for deep accents or text on light blue.
- Fog, Light Gray, and White are the main quiet surfaces.
- Text on Royal Blue is White.
- Text on light surfaces is Near Black or Body Gray.
- Warm decorative accents are prohibited.
- Neon colors are prohibited.
- Multicolor gradients are prohibited.
- Color is used for hierarchy and brand recognition, not decoration.

| Optional serif accent | None by default |

## 10. Typography Rules

- Maximum 2 font families per asset.
- Maximum 3 font weights per asset.
- Decorative fonts are prohibited.
- Long Russian text in all caps is prohibited.
- Technical label styling is prohibited.
- Code-style typography is prohibited as decoration.
- English abbreviations keep standard capitalization.
- Headlines are short and declarative.

## 11. Composition Rules

- The primary composition uses a clean light surface and a large rounded Royal Blue panel.
- The blue panel must feel calm, not aggressive.
- One visual unit contains one main idea.
- One visual unit contains one main focal point.
- Visual hierarchy must be readable within 1-2 seconds.
- White space is required.
- Decorative visual noise is prohibited.
- Text over noisy photography is prohibited.
- Elements without function are removed.
- Numeric grids, artificial system labels, and pseudo-interface decoration are prohibited.

## 19. Final QA

An asset is valid only if:

- one main idea is clear;
- hierarchy is readable in 1-2 seconds;
- Royal Blue is preserved as a brand signal;
- the composition preserves the clean blue-panel system;
- no visual noise is present;
- no pseudo logo is present;
- no numeric decoration is present;
- colors match the brand palette;
- typography is calm and readable;
- Russian text is primary;
- English abbreviations follow the rules;
- no text overlaps;
- no text sits on a noisy image;
- the result feels clean, aesthetic, calm, and engineering-first.
